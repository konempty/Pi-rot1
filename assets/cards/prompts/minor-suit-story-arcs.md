# Minor Suit Story Arcs

Use these arcs before generating each minor suit. Cards Ace through Ten should read as one visible story with cause, consequence, and time progression. Court cards may stand outside that plot and should prioritize their traditional court meaning.

## Wands

Status: generated and accepted through `35_wands_king.png`.

Protagonist: a dark violet-black-haired young maker in a deep purple star-lined cloak with a gold Pi-emblem chest medallion.

Core arc: a purple-haired maker discovers living creative fire, shares it, proves it, defends it, turns responsibility into a guild beacon, and leaves a lineage of creative leadership.

Element: fire, shown subtly through warm rim light, ember motes, safe wand sparks, and controlled creative heat.

### Ace Through Ten

1. Ace of Wands: Spark. At dawn, the maker discovers one living wand in an empty guild workshop valley. Creative fire first awakens.
2. Two of Wands: Planning. In early morning, the maker studies a world orb and Pi compass between two planning beacons. The vision expands beyond the valley.
3. Three of Wands: Outreach. In mid-morning, three cliffside beacon wands send message sparks toward airships and a violet sea route. The idea leaves home.
4. Four of Wands: First Beacon. Near noon, the maker and friends raise four wands into a small guild gate. Private spark becomes shared celebration.
5. Five of Wands: Friction. In bright afternoon, young makers clash training wands in friendly rivalry. Creative energy meets resistance.
6. Six of Wands: Recognition. In golden afternoon, the maker returns through town with a raised wand and six banners. Work becomes public victory.
7. Seven of Wands: Defense. In late afternoon, the maker defends the beacon from six challenge wands below. Leadership requires courage.
8. Eight of Wands: Momentum. At sunset, eight blazing message wands launch across the sky. The project moves quickly.
9. Nine of Wands: Resilience. At dusk, the tired maker guards nine planted wands around the repaired beacon. The vision survives pressure.
10. Ten of Wands: Responsibility. At heavy twilight, ten wands become a grand circular guild gate around the maker. Burden becomes dignified leadership.

## Cups

Status: generated and accepted through `49_cups_king.png`.

Protagonist: a gentle lavender-haired seer in a pearl-lavender cloak with a gold Pi-emblem cup pendant.

Core arc: the seer discovers a golden cup spring, learns trust and community, suffers emotional loss, recovers through memory and discernment, leaves shallow comfort to find the true source, then returns that water to a chosen-family festival.

Element: water, shown subtly through streams, fountains, reflections, droplets, mist, moonlit pools, and emotional flow.

### Ace Through Ten

1. Ace of Cups: Beginning. At spring morning, the seer discovers a golden cup overflowing at a hidden fountain. First emotional awakening.
2. Two of Cups: Bond. Later that morning, the seer shares the spring water with one trusted friend beside the stream. Mutual trust begins.
3. Three of Cups: Community. At midday, the seer and two companions bring the water to the village river. Friendship becomes shared celebration.
4. Four of Cups: Pause. In the quiet afternoon after the celebration, the seer notices emotional stillness and a fourth cup forming in mist. The story slows before trouble.
5. Five of Cups: Loss. Rain breaks the mood; three cups spill near the bridge while two still glow behind the seer. Grief arrives, but hope remains.
6. Six of Cups: Memory. After the rain, children and old memories help the seer refill six flower cups. Kindness restores the thread.
7. Seven of Cups: Temptation. At misty sunset, seven vision-cups rise from a mirror pool. The seer must choose reality over seductive illusions.
8. Eight of Cups: Departure. At moonrise, the seer leaves eight cups at the lakeshore and walks toward the mountain spring with a Pi lantern. A deeper search begins.
9. Nine of Cups: Inner Fulfillment. In the deep night, the seer finds the hidden moonwell and arranges nine filled cups. The wish is fulfilled privately first.
10. Ten of Cups: Shared Harmony. At sunrise, the seer returns the true source water to the river festival; ten cups arc over a chosen family and restored community.

### Court Cards

- Page of Cups: playful intuition, emotional surprise, a magical fish or message in a cup.
- Knight of Cups: romantic devotion, sincere offering, creative quest across water.
- Queen of Cups: compassion, deep intuition, protected emotional wisdom.
- King of Cups: calm emotional mastery, peace, and leadership amid moving water.

## Swords

Status: generated and accepted through `63_swords_king.png`.

Protagonist: a silver-haired sky-temple student in a pale blue-violet cloak with a gold Pi-emblem feather badge.

Core arc: the student awakens to truth at a sky temple, faces a difficult choice, learns that sharp truth can wound, rests and recovers, loses friends through hollow victory, leaves for calmer air, uses strategy, confronts self-imposed restriction and anxiety, then releases an old identity at pre-dawn.

Element: air, shown subtly through open sky, clouds, wind ribbons, feathers, drifting petals, clear atmosphere, and motion lines.

### Ace Through Ten

1. Ace of Swords: Awakening. In early mist, a single bright sword rises above the sky temple; the student first sees truth.
2. Two of Swords: Choice. At cool dawn, the blindfolded student sits at the temple gate with two crossed swords, torn between loyalty and truth.
3. Three of Swords: Consequence. Morning rain falls as three swords pierce a floating crystal heart; the student learns that truth and words can wound.
4. Four of Swords: Recovery. After rain, the student rests in a quiet sanctuary beneath four suspended swords and gathers clarity.
5. Five of Swords: Hollow Victory. In windy afternoon, the student wins a training dispute but classmates walk away; victory feels empty.
6. Six of Swords: Transition. In late-afternoon fog, the student leaves by small sky boat with six swords toward calmer shores.
7. Seven of Swords: Strategy. At sunset, the student arranges seven practice swords in a sky-temple strategy courtyard; clever planning replaces brute force, and cleverness still has consequences.
8. Eight of Swords: Restriction. At purple dusk, the student stands loosely bound among eight swords, realizing the path out was always open.
9. Nine of Swords: Anxiety. Deep night brings nine shadowy thought-blades over the bed; the student faces fear rather than running.
10. Ten of Swords: Release. At darkest pre-dawn, ten swords are laid around the student's fallen cloak, ending the old identity as sunrise begins.

### Court Cards

- Page of Swords: alert curiosity, honest observation, early mental agility.
- Knight of Swords: decisive action, intellectual speed, bold pursuit of truth.
- Queen of Swords: clear boundaries, precise compassion, honest wisdom.
- King of Swords: fair judgment, mastered clarity, ethical authority.

## Pentacles

Status: generated and accepted through `77_pentacles_king.png`.

Protagonist: an auburn-haired young merchant-gardener in an emerald-and-purple cloak with a gold Pi-emblem coin satchel.

Core arc: the merchant receives a single Pi coin seed, learns to balance resources, builds value with collaborators, becomes too protective of early success, passes through hardship, restores fairness through generosity, waits for planted value to grow, masters craft through repetition, enjoys earned independence, then turns prosperity into a lasting multi-generation city legacy.

Element: earth, shown subtly through soil, gardens, roots, stone, market produce, minerals, harvest light, coin seeds, and grounded textures.

### Ace Through Ten

1. Ace of Pentacles: Seed. In spring morning, a garden cloud offers one large Pi-emblem coin seed above fertile soil. Opportunity is planted.
2. Two of Pentacles: Balance. In breezy spring, the merchant juggles two Pi coins between market boats and seed trays, learning resource flow.
3. Three of Pentacles: Collaboration. In late spring, the merchant, artisan, and architect set three Pi coins into a workshop arch. Skill becomes shared structure.
4. Four of Pentacles: Clinging. At early summer, first profit makes the merchant guard four coins too tightly in the market. Security becomes fear.
5. Five of Pentacles: Hardship. Summer rain leaves the merchant and friend outside a glowing coin-window. Scarcity appears, but companionship remains.
6. Six of Pentacles: Fair Exchange. After rain, the merchant shares six coins with neighbors using balanced scales. Value begins circulating again.
7. Seven of Pentacles: Patience. In midsummer, seven coin fruits grow on vines; the merchant pauses to evaluate slow work.
8. Eight of Pentacles: Mastery. In late summer, the merchant engraves eight Pi coins at a workbench. Repetition turns value into craft.
9. Nine of Pentacles: Earned Grace. In early autumn, the merchant rests in a vineyard garden with nine coin fruits, self-sufficient and refined.
10. Ten of Pentacles: Legacy. At autumn festival, ten Pi coins form an arch over a multi-generation market city. Prosperity becomes shared inheritance.

### Court Cards

- Page of Pentacles: practical learning, study, a single tangible opportunity.
- Knight of Pentacles: patience, reliability, steady work, careful progress.
- Queen of Pentacles: nurturing stewardship, practical care, embodied abundance.
- King of Pentacles: grounded mastery, mature prosperity, generous material leadership.
